## 🛠️ How It Works (Setup in 60s)

Getting started with Gitdoro takes less than a minute. Turn your raw effort into undeniable, quantifiable progress!

![Repo Overview](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/viniscodes/gitdoro-ide-time-tracker/main/repo_overview.png)

### 1. Connect your Account
After installing the extension from the VS Code Marketplace, look at the bottom right of your VS Code window in the **Status Bar**.
1. Click `Gitdoro: Connect`.
2. Fast and secure browser authorization will link your IDE.
3. Once authorized, return to VS Code. The status bar will show your timer ready at `▶ 00:00`.

### 2. Enter Flow State (Start Tracking)
When you are ready to enter flow state and track your code time:
1. Click the `▶ 00:00` timer on the status bar.
2. The timer ticks (`▶ 00:01`, `▶ 00:02`...). You are now in a focus session!
3. Gitdoro automatically associates this session with your current workspace.

### 3. Pause or Stop a Session
Need a break or finished for the day?
1. Click the running timer (e.g., `▶ 25:00`) on the status bar.
2. A menu will appear at the top of VS Code with options:
   - **Pause Timer**: Temporarily halts the timer. You can resume anytime.
   - **Stop Tracking**: Ends the session completely and finalizes the sync to your Gitdoro dashboard.
   - **Open Dashboard**: Takes you directly to your stats on [gitdoro.com](https://gitdoro.com).

## 📊 View Your Progress & Stats

Click **Open Dashboard** from the menu or visit your Gitdoro profile to see your newly tracked time added to your daily streak, total XP, and project history! High retention, gamification, and stats keep you motivated to code every day.

## ⌨️ Commands

Control Gitdoro easily via the VS Code Command Palette (`Ctrl+Shift+P` or `Cmd+Shift+P`):
- `Gitdoro: Start Timer`
- `Gitdoro: Pause Timer`
- `Gitdoro: Stop Timer`
- `Gitdoro: Login`
- `Gitdoro: Open Dashboard`

## 🔒 Privacy & Security

We only track the focus time you explicitly record or configure the extension to track. Your data is synced securely to your Gitdoro account. **We do not track keystrokes, read your code, or access your private repositories.**
